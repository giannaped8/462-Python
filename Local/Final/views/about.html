About Us

This project was developed by Gianna Pedicini and Isabella Lombardo, senior undergraduate students pursuing Bachelor of Science degrees in Computer Engineering at Washington University in St. Louis, Class of 2025. Completed under the advisory of Dr. Michael Hall, this work represents the final deliverable for our senior capstone project in the McKelvey School of Engineering.

As part of our capstone requirement, we were tasked with applying hardware acceleration to an algorithm of our choosing. Drawing on our interests in cybersecurity, embedded systems, and educational accessibility, we chose the Advanced Encryption Standard (AES)â€”a widely used encryption algorithm critical to modern digital security.

The result is this interactive, educational website designed to demystify AES encryption for a broad audience. The platform allows users to input plaintext, initiate AES encryption, and observe the transformation in real time. To simulate practical performance improvements, the encryption process is offloaded to a PYNQ Z2 FPGA development board, demonstrating the role of hardware acceleration in cryptographic systems.

The website is the culmination of a semester-long effort and follows a structured development path:

  • Milestone 1 involved establishing communication between the front-end and the FPGA via a REST API and verifying data transfer between the microprocessor and hardware.
  • Milestone 2 focused on implementing key AES modules on the FPGA, supported by a software-based model for verification.
  • The final deliverable, presented here, integrates these components into a complete AES encryption pipeline, paired with intuitive, interactive visualizations for educational use.

By bridging the gap between theoretical cryptography and hardware-level implementation, our website serves as a tool for learners to gain a deeper, hands-on understanding of secure data systems and the growing importance of hardware acceleration in the field.
