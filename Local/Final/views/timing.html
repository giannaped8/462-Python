Timing Optimization of Hardware Acceleration

As part of our original project goals, we planned to analyze and optimize the timing performance of AES encryption on the PYNQ Z2 board. This would have involved detailed measurements of execution latency, comparison between software and hardware implementations, and potential pipeline optimizations to reduce processing time. However, due to the limited duration of our senior capstone, one semester, we prioritized the successful implementation and demonstration of the AES encryption process, both functionally and educationally. While timing optimization remains an important aspect of hardware acceleration, it ultimately fell beyond the scope of our available timeline. This page serves as a placeholder for future expansion, should this project be continued or used as a foundation for further research into performance tuning and optimization strategies on FPGA platforms.
